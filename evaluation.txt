evaluation.txt — Critical Evaluation of AI-Generated .pylintrc
===============================================================

WHAT THE AI GOT RIGHT
The AI correctly identified that numpy, pandas, and scikit-learn generate a huge
number of false-positive E1101 (no-member) errors because pylint cannot resolve
their C-extension attributes statically. Disabling E1101 and adding those
packages to generated-members was exactly the right call — without this, the
score would tank on every legitimate numpy array operation. The decision to set
max-line-length=88 to align with Black was also smart, since Black and pylint
would otherwise constantly disagree when used together in the same project.

WHAT I WOULD CHANGE
The AI disabled W0611 (unused-import) entirely, which is too lenient. Beginners
genuinely benefit from being told when they imported something they never used —
it is a real habit to build. I would re-enable this rule and instead teach
students to use a `# noqa` comment on specific deliberate imports. I would also
lower max-args from 8 to 6, which is a more standard professional threshold.

RULES TOO STRICT FOR BEGINNERS
C0114 (missing-module-docstring) is legitimately too strict at the learning stage.
Every small script does not need a module-level docstring, and forcing one causes
frustration without teaching anything meaningful compared to function docstrings.

RULES TOO LENIENT
Disabling R0903 (too-few-public-methods) may hide a real design problem where
beginners write bloated classes with a single method that should just be a
function. I would leave this enabled with a note explaining the warning rather
than silencing it entirely.

OVERALL VERDICT
The AI config is a solid 80% starting point — production-ready with a few tweaks.
The critical skill learned is that AI configs must always be reviewed against the
specific project context rather than accepted blindly.
